## 7. Compliance notes — Meta personal-attributes policy

Meta prohibits ads that assert or imply knowledge of a person's personal attributes — including religion, beliefs, family status, and parental status. For Gadugy this means:

- **Talk about the product, not the reader.** "Gadugy is a community for values-driven homeschool families" is fine. "Are you a Christian homeschooler?" or "As a faith-driven parent, you…" is not.
- **No "you/your" attribute call-outs.** Avoid second-person phrasing that names beliefs or identity: "your faith," "your values as a conservative family," "Christian parents like you." Describe the community's character instead ("families who share your hopes" is acceptable because it refers to matching, not to a protected attribute).
- **Religion stays implied, never targeted.** We never name a faith, denomination, or political identity in ad copy or targeting. "Values-driven" and "character-first" are our whole vocabulary.
- **Parental status is sensitive too.** "Homeschool families" describes the product's purpose. Frame it as what Gadugy is for ("a community for LA homeschool families"), not who the reader is ("You're a homeschool mom, so…").
- **Targeting hygiene.** Use interest/behavior targeting (homeschooling, parenting, LA geography) rather than copy that calls out the attribute. Let the landing page do the filtering.
- **Children's privacy.** Never show identifiable children or child names in creatives. Illustrations, park scenes, and the map work better anyway — and match the site's own privacy promises.
- **Before/after framing.** Avoid "tired of being the only homeschool family you know?" style loneliness call-outs aimed at the reader; keep the same sentiment product-side ("homeschooling in LA can feel lonely" describes the situation, not the person).
