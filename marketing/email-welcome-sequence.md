## E3 — How matching + safety/review works (Day 5)

**Subject:** How Gadugy actually works (and how we keep it safe)
**Preview text:** Reviewed families, private profiles, messaging only after a mutual yes.

Hi [First name],

A few of you asked how matching works, so here's the honest rundown.

**1. You tell us about your family.** Your homeschool stage, kids' ages, education hopes, values, and what kind of community you're looking for. You control what's visible — some answers are used only for matching and never shown on your profile.

**2. Every family is reviewed by a real person.** No open signups. ID verification helps us confirm that real local parents are behind each profile — and your ID is never shown to other families. This is what keeps Gadugy parent-only.

**3. We suggest compatible families nearby.** Matching looks at what actually shapes community fit: shared values, education philosophy, faith preferences, kids' ages, your LA area, and technology attitudes. Early matches are human-reviewed, because trust matters more than speed.

**4. You connect only when both families say yes.** Send a request; if they accept, messaging opens. If not, nothing happens. Child names stay private by default, and exact addresses are never shown.

The goal is fewer, better-fit connections — not endless browsing.

If any of that raises a question, just reply. I answer personally.

Warmly,
[Founder name]
