# Gadugy Community Outreach Templates

**Purpose:** Scrappy, permission-first outreach scripts for grassroots distribution. Every message leads with value (the free guide), asks permission, and never spams. Personalize the [brackets] every time — copy-paste blasting is how you get banned from groups.

**Golden rules:**
- Always message the admin/leader BEFORE posting in any group.
- Offer the free guide as the value, not Gadugy itself. Gadugy rides along on the guide's back page.
- Follow each group's self-promo rules exactly (some have designated days).
- One follow-up max, 5–7 days later. Silence is an answer.

---

## A. Facebook group admin outreach (3 variants)

### Variant 1 — The straightforward ask (general LA homeschool groups)

> Hi [Name]! I'm a local parent in [neighborhood] and I put together a free PDF called *The LA Homeschool Community Starter Guide* — 25 places across LA (parks, libraries, nature centers, free museum days) where families can meet other homeschool-minded families, even before they're officially homeschooling. No cost, no catch — I made it because finding community here was harder than I expected when we started exploring.
>
> Before I post anything, I wanted to check: would it be okay to share it with the group? Happy to send you the PDF first so you can see exactly what it is. And of course, if it's not a fit for the group, no worries at all.
>
> Thanks for all you do to keep this community running — I know it's work. 🌱
>
> [Your first name]

### Variant 2 — The member-first ask (you've been in the group a while)

> Hi [Name]! I've been a member here since [rough time] and have really appreciated [something specific — "the park day threads," "how welcoming everyone is to newbies"].
>
> I recently made a free resource I think this group would genuinely like: a starter guide to 25 spots around LA where homeschool-curious families can meet people in real life — libraries, nature centers, park days, free museum days, organized by neighborhood cluster (Pasadena, South Bay, the Valley, Glendale/Burbank, Long Beach, Santa Clarita, Westside).
>
> It's totally free, and I'd love to share it here if that's okay with you. I can send it over first so you can look it over — and I'm happy to post it in whatever format fits the group rules best (a comment, a file, a designated promo day, whatever you prefer).
>
> No pressure either way — thanks for considering it!
>
> [Your first name]

### Variant 3 — The short & warm ask (small/very active groups, or groups with strict promo rules)

> Hi [Name]! Quick question — I made a free guide for local families: *25 places in LA to meet homeschool-minded families (before you ever join a co-op)*. Parks, libraries, nature centers — all free spots, organized by neighborhood.
>
> Would it be alright to share it here? Glad to send it to you first, or post it on a promo day if that's the group norm. Either way, thanks for running this space. 🙏
>
> [Your first name]

## B. Co-op leaders & park-day organizers (2 variants)

### Variant 1 — Co-op leader (email or DM)

> Subject: A free resource for your families (and your waitlist)
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> I'm a homeschool[-ing / -exploring] parent in [neighborhood], and I've heard wonderful things about [co-op name] — [one specific detail if you have it, e.g., "especially the nature study mornings"].
>
> I put together a free PDF called *The LA Homeschool Community Starter Guide*: 25 places across LA where families who are considering homeschooling can start meeting people — before they're ready to commit to a co-op. It's organized by neighborhood cluster and everything in it is free or nearly free.
>
> Two reasons I thought of you:
>
> 1. **Your families** might enjoy it as a "what's near us" reference for the off weeks.
> 2. **Your waitlist/inquirers** — the families who email you mid-year or aren't ready to join yet — might find it a kind, useful thing to receive instead of a "sorry, we're full."
>
> I'd be happy to send you the PDF to look over. If you like it, feel free to forward it, print a stack, or drop it in your newsletter — whatever serves your people. There's a small note about my project (Gadugy, a private matching community for LA homeschool families) on the very last page, but the guide itself is 100% useful-content, zero pitch.
>
> Thanks for building what you're building. Communities like yours are why LA homeschool families find each other at all.
>
> Warmly,
> [Your first name]
> [phone, optional] • hello@gadugy.com

### Variant 2 — Park-day / standing-meetup organizer (DM or in person)

> Hi [Name]! I love what you've built with [park day name] — we came [last week / a friend told me about it] and it was exactly the kind of low-pressure morning new homeschool families hope to find.

I'd love to include your meetup in the [cluster] chapter, and to send you a few printed copies (with a QR code) you can hand out to new families who show up asking "where else do people meet?"

> Thanks again for holding this space every week. It matters more than it probably feels like some mornings. 🌱
>
> [Your first name] • hello@gadugy.com

> **Get the free guide →** [QR CODE]

>
> *[small footer] Brought to you by Gadugy — a private community for values-driven LA homeschool families.*

**Print note:** Print ~20 at a time (library printing works in a pinch, ~$0.10–0.15/page). Keep a few in a folder in the diaper bag / backpack. Hand them out when a conversation naturally turns to "we're thinking about it" — the QR code does the rest.
